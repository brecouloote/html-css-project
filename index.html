COVID-19 Testing in the Bronx

By Breona Couloote
October 5, 2020

Photo Credit: United States Naval Academy Photo Archive

Out of all the boroughs in New York State, the Bronx experienced the most death from the COVID-19 virus. According to NYC Health, more than 2,000 Bronx residents died from COVID. With the anticipation of a “second-wave” of the virus, it is important to know what steps to take if someone thinks they contracted COVID-19.


Symptoms

According to the CDC, mild symptoms include fever, chills, coughing, shortness of breath, difficulty breathing, fatigue, body aches, headaches, loss of taste or smell, sore throat, congestion, a runny nose, nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea. In addition, more severe symptoms include trouble breathing, persistent pain, pressure in the chest, confusion, inability to wake or stay awake and blue lips or face. These symptoms usually appear 2 through 14 days after being to someone who is COVID positive. If you experience a combination of these symptoms, please call your primary doctor. However if you do not have a primary doctor, you can still visit a testing center.


Finding a Testing Clinic

If you are located in the Bronx, all NYC Health + Hospital locations include COVID-19 testing. There are numerous other health facilities, such as CityMD and CVS, where you can also receive a test. To find the nearest testing site, please click this link.


The Test

Once you arrive to a testing site, the COVID-19 test is pretty quick. Although there are two different types of tests, the nasal swab test is the most common. A practitioner will swipe the swab along the sides of each nostril for 15 seconds. Depending on if you receive a rapid test, your test results will be known anywhere from an hour to three days. If you test positive, please isolate yourself for at least 14 days and monitor your conditions. If symptoms become worst, seek emergency care. To learn more about testing, please click here.
